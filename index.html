How RailSeva works

A step-by-step guide to filing, tracking, and understanding your Indian Railways AI grievance.

📋 The Complete Process
1
🎟️
Enter Your PNR
Type your 10-digit PNR number. The system verifies it against the database and auto-fills your journey — train name, route, class, date, and passenger count.
2
🎙 / ✍️
Speak or Type
Record a voice complaint OR type it out in any of the 22 official Indian languages. No language selection required — Gemini detects it automatically.
3
🤖
Gemini AI Analyses
Google Gemini 1.5 Flash receives the raw audio or text. It transcribes, detects the language, translates to English, classifies into a category, and writes an admin summary — all automatically.
4
🗄️
Saved to Database
Your complaint is saved to the RailSeva SQLite database with a unique ticket number (format: RC-YYYYMMDD-XXXXXXX). This is used for tracking.
5
📧
Forwarded to Authority
An email is automatically sent from [email protected] to the responsible department — staff, security, hygiene, electrical, or vigilance — based on the AI classification.
6
🔍
Track Your Complaint
Use the Track Status tab with your ticket number to check the current status: Pending, In Progress, or Resolved. The full complaint record is shown.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Which languages are supported? ▼
All 22 scheduled Indian languages are supported: Hindi, Bengali, Telugu, Marathi, Tamil, Urdu, Gujarati, Kannada, Odia, Malayalam, Punjabi, Assamese, Maithili, Sanskrit, Kashmiri, Nepali, Sindhi, Konkani, Manipuri, Dogri, Bodo, and Santhali — plus English. You do not select a language; Gemini detects it automatically from your speech or text.
How does voice recording work? ▼
Tap the large red microphone button and speak naturally in any language. The raw audio is sent directly to Google Gemini 1.5 Flash. Gemini transcribes what was said verbatim, detects the language automatically, translates it to English, and classifies the complaint. You will see exactly what the AI heard and understood as a preview before you confirm submission.
What happens after I submit? ▼
Two things happen immediately: (1) Your complaint is saved to the RailSeva database with a unique ticket number like RC-20260323-ABCXYZ. (2) An automated email is sent from [email protected] to the responsible railway department based on AI classification. You can use the ticket number to track progress in the Track Status tab.
How do I track my complaint? ▼
Click the Track Status tab at the top. Enter your ticket number exactly as shown on the confirmation screen (format: RC-YYYYMMDD-XXXXXXX). Click Track and you will see your complaint details, classification, assigned authority, and current resolution status (Pending / In Progress / Resolved). Note: only complaints submitted while app.py was running are saved to the database.
What is a "Not Valid Complaint"? ▼
If the AI determines the submission is not a genuine Indian Railways grievance — for example a general question, a test message, or something unrelated to train travel — it will classify it as "Not Valid Complaint". No email will be sent and no database record will be created. You will be asked to re-describe a specific onboard incident.
What is a media mismatch? ▼
If you attach a photo or video as evidence, Gemini checks whether the visual content matches your complaint text. If you describe a dirty toilet but attach an unrelated image, the AI flags it as a mismatch and asks you to re-attach relevant evidence before submission.
Why does the email show as [email protected]? ▼
The system uses email masking: the actual sending account is a Gmail address, but the "From" field displayed to recipients is set to [email protected] for a professional appearance. All reply-to addresses go to the railway authority inboxes, not to individual passengers.

Frequently Asked Questions
Which languages are supported? ▼
All 22 scheduled Indian languages: Hindi, Bengali, Telugu, Marathi, Tamil, Urdu, Gujarati, Kannada, Odia, Malayalam, Punjabi, Assamese, Maithili, Sanskrit, Kashmiri, Nepali, Sindhi, Konkani, Manipuri, Dogri, Bodo, Santhali — plus English. You do not need to select your language; Gemini detects it automatically from your speech or text.
How does voice recording work? ▼
Click the microphone button and speak your complaint naturally in any language. The raw audio is sent to Gemini AI, which transcribes your speech, identifies the language, translates it to English, and then classifies the complaint. You will see a preview of what the AI understood before you submit.
Where does my complaint go after submission? ▼
Your complaint is first saved to the RailSeva database with a unique ticket number (e.g. RC-20260323-ABCXYZ). It is then automatically forwarded via email to the responsible authority based on category — staff issues go to [email protected], security issues to [email protected], electrical issues to [email protected], etc. The email appears to come from [email protected].
How do I track my complaint? ▼
Click the "Track Status" tab in the navigation bar. Enter your ticket number (shown on the confirmation screen, format RC-YYYYMMDD-XXXXXXX) and click Track. You will see the full complaint details, category, assigned authority, and current status.
What counts as a valid complaint? ▼
A valid complaint must relate to an onboard railway experience — coach conditions, staff behaviour, security incidents, electrical equipment, or corruption. Complaints unrelated to your train journey (e.g. general suggestions, ticket booking issues) will be flagged as "Not Valid Complaint" by the AI and will not be forwarded.
What is the Gemini API key for? ▼
The Gemini API key connects RailSeva to Google's Gemini 1.5 Flash model, which powers all AI classification, transcription, and translation. You can get a free key from aistudio.google.com. Without a valid key, the system falls back to a keyword-based classifier which works for basic complaints in English.
